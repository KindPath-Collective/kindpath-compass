# Emotional Architecture — Compass Prompt

Emotional architecture asks: what structure is holding this experience in place?

Not to be dismantled. To be understood. You cannot work responsively with
architecture you haven't read.

## Load-bearing elements to identify:

**Relational:** Who does this person believe they must be in order to be loved?
Who are they terrified of disappointing? Who gave them their first understanding
of what they were worth?

**Historical:** What has already been tried and failed? What does the person
already believe about their situation that shapes every new attempt? Where did
that belief come from?

**Systems:** What external structures (justice, housing, family law, mental health,
NDIS itself) are load-bearing? What does the person need to maintain with those
systems in order to keep access to things they need — and what does that maintenance
cost them?

**Survival architecture:** What behaviours, beliefs, or relational patterns that
look like problems are actually solutions to older problems? Addiction as affect
regulation. Aggression as boundary-setting with no other tools. Isolation as
the only way the person has found to be safe.

## The key question:

What would need to change — and what would have to be held steady — for genuine
movement to be possible here?

Architecture is not destiny. But trying to change behaviour without understanding
the architecture it rests on is how interventions fail and practitioners burn out.
