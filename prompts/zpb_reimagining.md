# ZPB Reimagining — Compass Prompt

ZPB = Zero-Point Benevolence

This is not optimism. It is not wishful thinking. It is not "what would happen
in a perfect world."

ZPB is a navigating direction, not a destination.

## The question:

What would this situation look like if **genuine care — not compliance, not
risk management, not budget constraints, not convenience, not the practitioner's
discomfort** — were the governing logic?

## How to use it:

1. Temporarily suspend the constraints (not to pretend they don't exist —
   to see past them to what's actually needed).

2. Describe the configuration you're moving toward. What does good look like here?
   What is the person actually capable of? What would support that does not
   extract from them look like?

3. Then — and this is where ZPB becomes practical — work backward from that
   vision to identify the first real step that moves toward it.

ZPB is not idealism. It is precision. The problem with most interventions is not
that they aim too high — it is that they aim in the wrong direction entirely
because they started from constraint rather than from what the person actually needs.

## The key trap:

"That's not realistic" is the most common way ZPB gets killed before it can be
useful. When that thought arrives, notice it. It is usually a fear, not a fact.
The constraints are real. But "realistic" often means "familiar to the system"
rather than "genuinely possible for this person."

Hold the vision. Work with the constraints. Don't collapse them together.
