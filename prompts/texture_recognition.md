# Texture Recognition — Compass Prompt

When reading texture, look for:

- What quality of experience is actually present? Not the label. Not the assessment.
  The lived texture of what this person is in.

- Is something wearing the clothes of something else?
  Common disguises to look for:
  — Grief as aggression (loss expressed as attack)
  — Fear as compliance (terror performing agreeableness)
  — Despair as indifference (giving up looks like not caring)
  — Loyalty as self-harm (staying because love, not because safe)
  — Intelligence as disruption (boredom in a system that can't hold it)
  — Overwhelm as aggression (the person who is too much is usually also too alone)

- What is the person actually asking for underneath what they're saying?
  Not the stated request. The need beneath it.

- What is the practitioner feeling in their body as they read this?
  Heaviness, constriction, helplessness, frustration, protectiveness?
  These are data. Not to be projected back — but to be named internally
  as part of reading what is present.

Texture recognition is not diagnosis. It is contact. Before anything else: make contact.
